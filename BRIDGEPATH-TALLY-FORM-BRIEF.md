# Create a Tally form: Bridgepath Parent Learning Survey

Create a concise, mobile-first research survey for parents and guardians of children in Standards 3, 4, and 5 in Trinidad and Tobago.

## Purpose

Learn how parents currently discover curriculum gaps, what support they use and pay for, and whether they would try a curriculum-based learning adventure. This is discovery research, not marketing. The survey should take no more than four minutes.

## Design rules

- Use one question per page and show a progress bar.
- Use plain Standard English and a warm, trustworthy tone.
- Do not ask for the child's name, school, grades, address, date of birth, or any other identifying child information.
- Make Questions 1–11 required.
- Limit free-form responses. Only the final optional follow-up contact field may accept text.
- Do not collect email addresses automatically and do not require respondents to sign in.
- Randomize answer choices only where explicitly requested.
- Do not use quiz scoring.
- Use a simple teal, warm yellow, cream, and coral visual theme if these styling controls are available.

## Welcome page

Title: Bridgepath Parent Learning Survey

Text: We are exploring a curriculum-based learning adventure for Trinidad and Tobago children in Standards 3–5. This anonymous survey takes about four minutes. Please do not provide your child's name or school.

Button: Begin

## Questions

### 1. What Standard is your child currently in?

Type: Multiple choice, one answer.

Options:
- Standard 3
- Standard 4
- Standard 5
- I have children in more than one of these Standards

### 2. How confident are you that you know which curriculum topics your child understands?

Type: Multiple choice, one answer.

Options:
- Very confident
- Somewhat confident
- Not very confident
- Not at all confident

### 3. How do you usually discover that your child is struggling with a topic?

Type: Multi-select. Allow more than one answer.

Options:
- Report card
- Conversation with the teacher
- Homework
- Tests or assessments
- Lessons teacher
- My child tells me
- I am usually unsure

### 4. When do you usually receive useful information about your child's progress?

Type: Multiple choice, one answer.

Options:
- Weekly
- Monthly
- At the end of the term
- Only when there is a problem
- Mainly during SEA preparation
- I rarely receive useful information

### 5. What additional learning support does your child currently use?

Type: Multi-select. Allow more than one answer.

Options:
- Help from a parent or family member
- Lessons provided by the school
- Private lessons
- Workbooks or printed practice papers
- Educational websites or apps
- No additional support

### 6. How frequently does your child attend extra academic lessons?

Type: Multiple choice, one answer.

Options:
- Almost every school day
- Two or three times per week
- Once per week
- Occasionally
- Never

### 7. Approximately how much does your family spend each month on additional academic support?

Type: Multiple choice, one answer.

Options:
- Nothing
- Under TT$200
- TT$200–499
- TT$500–999
- TT$1,000 or more
- Prefer not to say

### 8. What are the biggest problems with the learning support currently available?

Type: Multi-select. Require at least one and allow no more than two answers. Randomize the answer order.

Options:
- Learning gaps are identified too late
- There is too much test practice and not enough understanding
- The work is not personalised to my child
- It is difficult to know whether my child is improving
- My child loses interest
- It takes too much family time
- It is too expensive
- I do not have a major problem with the current support

### 9. Which Bridgepath benefits would be most valuable to your family?

Description: Bridgepath is a proposed learning adventure that would connect activities to the Trinidad and Tobago curriculum.

Type: Multi-select. Require at least one and allow no more than three answers. Randomize the answer order.

Options:
- Showing the exact curriculum topics my child needs help with
- Teaching lessons matched to those gaps
- Showing whether my child can apply a concept in a new situation
- Giving me a clear parent progress report
- Suggesting simple activities we can do at home
- Helping identify gaps before Standard 5
- Giving my child an adventure they want to continue

### 10. Would you consider trying Bridgepath with your child for four weeks?

Type: Multiple choice, one answer.

Options:
- Yes
- Maybe
- No

### 11. If Bridgepath helped your child close curriculum gaps, what monthly price would feel reasonable?

Type: Multiple choice, one answer.

Options:
- I would only use a free version
- Under TT$50
- TT$50–99
- TT$100–199
- TT$200 or more
- I am unsure

### 12. May we contact you for a 20-minute parent interview?

Type: Yes/no. Optional.

Options:
- Yes
- No

If the answer is Yes, show one optional short-text field:

Label: Preferred email address or phone number

Description: Your contact information will be used only to arrange this research conversation and should be kept separate from the anonymous survey analysis.

If the answer is No or the question is skipped, do not show the contact field.

## Thank-you page

Title: Thank you for helping shape Bridgepath

Text: Your experience will help us design a learning tool that gives families earlier, clearer information about curriculum understanding. Please do not display a score or public response summary.

## Final checks

- Keep the form as a draft after generation.
- Do not publish it automatically.
- Verify the maximum-selection limits on Questions 8 and 9.
- Verify the conditional contact field appears only after a Yes response to Question 12.
- Confirm anonymous responses are allowed without sign-in.
