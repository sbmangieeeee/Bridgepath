## Source handling

- Both files are image-based scans and do not contain a reliable searchable text layer.
- Curriculum claims must be checked visually against the source pages before they are treated as final mappings.
- Objective IDs, wording, examples, prerequisites, and evidence requirements must be approved by a qualified Trinidad and Tobago educator before publication.
- Bridgepath content must be original. The assessment is evidence about expected transfer, not a question bank or template.

## Working interpretation

The curriculum's five proficiency strands are foundational to Bridgepath:

1. conceptual understanding;
2. procedural fluency;
3. strategic competence;
4. adaptive reasoning; and
5. productive disposition.

This means coverage cannot be inferred from question completion alone. Bridgepath must teach what a concept means, help the child use it, require independent application, and collect evidence that the learner can explain or check the result.
