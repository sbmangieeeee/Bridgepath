Children call him **Niko**. His surname must not imply ethnicity, religion, class, occupation, or personality.

## Identity

Niko notices when everyone is still discussing and helps the team begin. He is warm, socially confident, practical, and quick to include someone new. He likes estimates, patterns, moving through places, meeting residents, and learning how things work.

His first ideas are plausible, not random. Mistakes come from acting too early, missing a unit or changed condition, or assuming a pattern continues. He learns to make momentum reliable without becoming less adventurous.

**Values:** courage to begin; people belong in the plan; ideas may change.

**Humour:** observational and self-aware. Never targets people or mistakes.

**Never write him as:** reckless comic relief, permanently loud/hyperactive, random guesser, always wrong, or fearless superhero.

## Relationship

Niko trusts Zuri's attention; she trusts his willingness to begin. She does not supervise him, and he does not pull her through every choice. Either may catch what the other missed.

Niko opens a possibility for the learner, then yields space. He may suggest an estimate, route, object, or person to ask. The learner chooses, builds, tests, or explains. He treats correction as useful information, not humiliation.

## Expression and movement

Niko's emotion arrives quickly and honestly. Excitement uses his whole posture; concentration draws energy inward. Disappointment is brief but real. Empathy lowers his energy to match the other person.

| State | Signature read |
|---|---|
| Curious | leading brow, forward lean, hand hovers without grabbing |
| Thinking | narrowed focus, centred weight, thumb near pouch strap |
| Confused | gaze between two features, open palm, step back to see whole |
| Determined | softened knees, settled strap, both hands ready |
| Proud | small smile; presents shared work, not himself |
| Celebrating | one grounded hop/heel lift, then opens to team/world |
| Disappointed | shoulders dip, hand steadies pouch, remains connected |
| Encouraging | crouches beside work, soft brow, palm up |
| Playful | side glance and crooked grin; tongue never visible; never during distress |

He accelerates quickly but stops with weight. Idle uses breath, gaze, a foot shift, and rare pouch adjustment. Mistake: stop → find missed condition → breathe → nod → check. Waiting never shows impatience. Reduced motion uses open-hand and eye-line pose changes.

**Reusable poses:** first step; quick estimate; stop-and-check; ask resident; map scout; try-my-idea then yield; “I missed that” without facepalm; carry together; quiet reset; team celebration.

## Writing and learning behaviour

- Short, active, inclusive Standard English, usually 4–10 words.
- Leans toward verbs, possibilities, estimates, and people.
- Admits uncertainty without comic panic.
- If every line has an exclamation mark, the voice is wrong.

Niko may identify what is happening, who needs help, or what could be tested first. He does not teach the concept or repeat instructions.

Useful mistakes: incomplete pattern, missing unit/label, weak estimate benchmark, first operation suggested by a word problem, or missed changed condition. Across a chapter set he must also notice correctly, check Zuri's assumption, act patiently, and receive help.

Encouragement names what the team now knows and offers a smaller action. Celebration points to the community result, not points or speed.

## Final check

- Invites action without taking the learner's action.
- First idea is plausible and useful.
- Sometimes notices/checks correctly before Zuri.
- Works without a grin or exaggerated movement.
- Curls, coral/teal, pouch, and forward stance remain intact.
- Humour preserves dignity; revision has no shame.
- Mentor and learner remain necessary.
