# Research Record

Accessed 14 July 2026. Public documentation, product pages, and reviews were examined. Firsthand signed-in competitive walkthroughs remain incomplete because the in-app browser failed during initialization; claims below are therefore labelled by source type.

| Product/source | Evidence | Adapt | Avoid |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Duolingo method](https://blog.duolingo.com/duolingo-teaching-method/) | Official product explanation | Short sessions, obvious next action, immediate feedback | Letting streaks or repetition substitute for understanding |

| [Khan Academy child accounts](https://support.khanacademy.org/hc/en-us/sections/4404527006221-Setting-up-and-Managing-Child-Accounts) | Official support documentation | Parent-managed restricted profiles | Dense, course-centric parent reporting |

| [Bookworm Adventures](https://www.mobygames.com/game/25295/bookworm-adventures/) | Game database and historical description | Chapters, escalation, academic actions driving outcomes | Punitive repetition and copied combat framing |

## Findings

- Use a hybrid illustrated map with semantic HTML controls and a list alternative.
- Let community characters teach concepts they naturally use.
- Preserve instruction, hints, and remediation even when a game challenge is lost.
- Keep narration optional, replayable, and text-equivalent.
- Separate evidence stage, curriculum coverage, adventure state, and rewards.
- Authenticate the parent; issue a constrained child session under a family relationship.
- Represent Trinidad and Tobago through ordinary architecture, vegetation, market life, transport, family, sport, and speech—not decorative stereotypes.

## Originality guardrails

Do not reproduce reference characters, copy, screen composition, icons, path geometry, sounds, reward vocabulary, or proprietary content. The Storypath visual asset in this repository was generated specifically for Bridgepath from an original prompt and contains no logos or reference characters.
