# Bridgepath Character Visual Validation

## Status

The first Niko turnaround is the approved visual reference for facial design, age, proportions, curl construction, illustration warmth, and everyday character treatment. The Design Lab uses it without redesigning the character. The first Zuri turnaround is used as the closest matching development reference, but her everyday styling still requires founder review.

All expression, pose, and context-wardrobe plates are clearly labelled construction placeholders. They validate range, layout, and reuse requirements; they are not final character drawings.

## Visual-validation checklist

- [ ] Both characters read as approximately nine years old.
- [ ] Niko and Zuri feel equal in scale, finish, agency, and narrative importance.
- [ ] Their silhouettes remain distinct without facial detail or colour.
- [ ] Coral/teal and violet/mango remain distinguishable at avatar size.
- [ ] Each character remains recognisable in front, three-quarter, profile, and rear views.
- [ ] Both children feel equally desirable and complete as the selected explorer.
- [ ] The unselected character reads as an equal companion, never the default teacher.
- [ ] Poses communicate companionship rather than instruction or supervision.
- [ ] Props visibly contact hands and stay on their assigned side.
- [ ] Emotional reactions never shame, rush, or mock the learner.
- [ ] No Niko or Zuri expression uses a visible tongue or tongue-out gesture.
- [ ] Both characters feel natural in the approved Storypath world.
- [ ] Hair, skin, wardrobe, and accessories pass local cultural review.
- [ ] The painterly treatment remains readable on light and dark UI surfaces.

## Contradictions and unresolved differences

1. **Modernity is now a wardrobe question, not a rendering question.** Earlier feedback could have been interpreted as changing the entire illustration style. Founder clarification locks the first-version face, proportions, and painterly warmth; only clothing should receive a separate contemporary refinement.
2. **Zuri's development image includes extra styling not defined as signature.** The first matching turnaround includes a waist textile, jewellery, and additional accessories. The bibles prioritise twin textured puffs, violet top, mango trousers, and a compact roll-top backpack. The extra items should not become permanent without explicit approval and task-safety review.
3. **Niko's approved image has a detailed explorer outfit.** The coral overshirt, teal centre, cross-body pouch, curls, and proportions are locked. The cargo-short cut, sock treatment, shoe profile, and pouch bulk may be modernised only in a separate wardrobe plate, without changing those identity anchors.
4. **Independent turnaround sheets do not establish a reliable shared height scale.** Final model sheets must place both characters on one baseline and lock their relative eye line, head size, hand size, and shoulder height.
5. **Proposed full names remain pending local review.** No surname should enter final art labels or public-facing copy until approved.

## Founder approvals needed before final character art

1. Confirm that the selected first Niko turnaround is the rendering and proportion master.
2. Confirm that Zuri should use the same rendering warmth and age treatment, while her everyday outfit is simplified and modernised.
3. Approve or remove Zuri's waist textile, earrings, bracelets, and other non-signature accessories.
4. Approve one modern everyday wardrobe plate for each character while preserving the locked colour blocks and carry-line silhouettes.
5. Approve the pair's exact relative height and shared model-sheet scale.
6. Approve how much painterly texture survives in 40–64 px avatars and lesson crops.
7. Complete Trinidad and Tobago cultural review of hair, clothing, bags, footwear, and contextual wardrobes before production illustration.

## Approved explorer model

For the first release, the child selects Niko or Zuri as their playable explorer. The unselected character becomes the adventure companion. Progress remains attached to the child profile, and the selected character may be changed later through an approved profile setting. Local mentor characters—not the companion—introduce and demonstrate curriculum concepts. The content system must support both explorer configurations without producing different learning outcomes.

## Browser validation

The Design Lab was checked at 1440×1000, 834×1112, and 390×844. It produced no horizontal overflow, no persistent broken images, and no browser console errors. The Niko/Zuri comparison control correctly swaps the turnaround, recognition anchors, and accessible image description.
