# Bridgepath Product Definition

## Promise

**Understand it, use it, and carry it into the adventure.** Bridgepath teaches curriculum concepts through community life before asking children to perform independently.

## Users and problem

Children in Standards 3–5 need conceptual instruction, meaningful application, practice, and confidence—not question banks alone. Parents need plain-language evidence of curriculum coverage, application, and support needs. Parents own child profiles; teachers are anticipated but outside the pilot.

## Learning philosophy

The core loop is `discover → learn → try with help → practise → apply → reflect → resume`. Questions support learning but are not the experience. Mistakes trigger feedback and scaffolding without removing access. Academic evidence is separate from adventure progress and rewards.

Evidence progresses through `introduced`, `guided`, `practised`, `applied`, and `mastered`. Mastery requires successful independent evidence in multiple contexts; a single score cannot produce it.

Bridgepath uses memorable models to reveal meaning, not replace it. A story, object, or mnemonic introduces the idea; the learner then connects concrete, pictorial, verbal, and symbolic representations, works without the cue, explains or checks the result, and applies the concept in a different context. Every chapter must make the mathematics necessary to resolving a believable community problem.

## Pilot scope

The responsive-web pilot contains family setup, child entry, playable orientation, Storypath country and town navigation, one complete curriculum-validated vertical slice, teaching, guided use, practice, remediation, independent application, saved progress, and parent reporting. The exact application context is selected after Curriculum Discovery approval; Market Day remains a brainstorming example rather than an approved slice.

The production pilot additionally requires real parent authentication, approved adult verification, consent records, Supabase persistence and authorization, export/deletion operations, support, and monitoring.

## Future vision

Extend Standard 3 Mathematics first, then English and Standards 4–5. Add teachers, diagnostics, richer worlds, optional contained game systems, and a CMS only after the learning and evidence systems have been validated.
