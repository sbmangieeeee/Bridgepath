Children call her **Zuri**. Her surname must not imply ethnicity, religion, class, occupation, or personality.

## Identity

Zuri notices when labels disagree, patterns change, or someone is missing from a “fair” plan. She is observant, composed, imaginative, and quietly expressive. She likes maps, design, measurements, schedules, clues, and systems with exceptions.

She organises because she cares what happens when a plan meets real people and materials. Mistakes come from waiting for unnecessary certainty, fixing categories too early, or keeping a neat plan after conditions change. She learns to act and adapt without becoming less thoughtful.

**Values:** careful attention; plans include people; order may change.

**Humour:** dry, visual, and precisely timed. Correction is never the joke.

**Never write her as:** perfect, permanently correct, Niko's supervisor, timid, fragile, humourless, desk-bound, or “bossy girl.”

## Relationship

Zuri values Niko's ability to make a problem approachable; he values her ability to reveal what a first move missed. Sometimes she starts the quick experiment; sometimes he finds her missing condition. Neither habit owns correctness.

Zuri may arrange two representations, reveal a mismatch, or ask whose need the plan serves. She then clears the interaction space. The learner selects, tests, reorganises, or explains.

## Expression and movement

Zuri's feelings build gradually but remain visible. Curiosity starts with focused stillness; delight opens her posture. Frustration may tighten her organisation before she names it. She listens before fixing and can celebrate with full physical joy.

| State | Signature read |
|---|---|
| Curious | angled brow, focused lids, still body, left hand frames object |
| Thinking | gaze moves among parts, fingers mark positions, weight anchors |
| Confused | eyes revisit mismatch, hands hold two possibilities apart |
| Determined | feet plant, hair/sleeves secure, left hand begins one action |
| Proud | contained smile grows; presents work and includes team |
| Celebrating | two-foot spring/quick turn, then grounded team hold |
| Disappointed | releases rigid grip or sets plan down; shifts to adaptation |
| Encouraging | clears clutter, comes to eye level, offers one choice |
| Playful | lifted brow, absurd-detail glance, restrained grin; tongue never visible |

She looks and aligns before one clear action. Idle uses breath, gaze, and a small strap/heel shift. Mistake: find assumption → loosen fingers → exhale → rearrange. Stillness never means loading. Waiting never becomes anxious checking. Reduced motion uses gaze and grounded pose changes.

**Reusable poses:** something changed; reveal the pattern; whose plan; enough to begin; map layers; check condition then yield; revise category; listen fully; carry carefully; joyful release.

## Writing and learning behaviour

- Concise Standard English: observation followed by open possibility.
- Usually 5–12 words; leans toward relationships, conditions, patterns, and fairness.
- Does not narrate every thought or always speak last.
- If every line corrects someone, the voice is wrong.

Zuri may identify a mismatch, condition, relationship, or fairness question. She does not teach the concept or pre-solve the task.

Useful mistakes: fixed/overlapping categories, equal shares without context, over-trusting a neat diagram, waiting for certainty, following an outdated plan, or missing a simpler test. Across a chapter set she must initiate action, take risks, be playful, revise her models, and accept Niko's or the learner's valid approach.

Encouragement preserves valid work and reduces the current decision. Celebration recognises reliability, fairness, explanation, and community use, not neatness alone.

## Final check

- Reveals structure without giving the answer.
- Initiates action and spontaneous play.
- Makes and revises plausible mistakes.
- Treats fairness as contextual, not a slogan.
- Twin puffs, violet/gold, backpack, and grounded stance remain intact.
- Humour exists without correction becoming the joke.
- Accepts others' approaches without supervising them.
- Mentor and learner remain necessary.
